Synopsis

The return of the The Return of the Skeleton Man by Joseph Bruchac is the second book to the sequel Skeleton man, continuing the thrilling story of Molly, a young mohawk girl. After surviving a terrifying encounter with the formidable skeleton man, Molly tries to return to a normal life. However, she begins experiencing eerie dreams and a growing sense of danger, realizing that the evil figure from her past may not be gone for good. With the help of her parents, hero instinct, and her connections to native traditions, Molly must face new challenges and uncover the truth before it's too late. The novel blends suspense with cultural folklore, keeping the reader on edge.

Quotes

Quote 1 : "And my dad, who I used to think was big and strong and smart enough to defeat any moster in the world, had been helpless."(69, Bruchac)

This quote stood out to me because it showed how although your parents, especially your father, may appear to be strong and indestructible, they are just humans with weaknesses at the end of the day.

Quote 2 : ,"No. Everything is fine. Just fine" (67, Bruchac)

Holly, who is the person that says this quote, is put in an extremely detreimental situation but cannot accept her fate and reality so she tries her best to look on the brighter side. Many people try to look on brighter side of thier miseries but a big part of trying to be optimistic is to accept your situation.

Quote 3 : "In the stark gleam of the headlights i can see the tall, thin figure astride that ATV, his head a glistening skull, his eyes red as blood" ( 126, Bruchac)

This quote demostrated great imagery that really described how Holly felt in that moment.

I would not recommend The return of the Skeleton Man by Joseph Bruchac primarily due to its slow pacing and underwhelming suspense. While the story has potential, the tension never fully builds, making it less engaging for readers who expect a thrilling mystery. In addition , the character lacks depth, and the plot feels predictable which detracts from the overall experience. For those seeking a gripping, fast-paced story, this book might feel disappointing and fail to hold attention.
